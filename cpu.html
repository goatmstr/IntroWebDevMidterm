CPU

The CPU or Central Processing Unit is perhaps the most important component in a PC. It is a necessary component of every single PC as it is what controls and processes every single thing the computer does. In a sense, the CPU is the computer itself. Without the CPU, the PC is simply an assortment of parts connected together that does nothing. In the scope of a gaming PC, the CPU is crucial in calculating the millions of calculations and instructions that needs to be done every second the game is running. Every little interaction, every bit of physics and game mechanics is quickly calculated by the many cores in the powerhouse that is the CPU. Generally, the most powerful and expensive CPUs on the market is overkill for gaming as those are used for heavy tasks done by workstations. Right now, the market is dominated by Intel's and AMD Ryzen's Processors when it comes to PC GPUs. Choosing between Intel and AMD is a question many PC enthusiasts debate about. But when it comes to price vs gaming performance, many would choose AMD over Intel as of 2024, specifically with the Ryzen 7 7800X3D which would be my personal recommendation.

The Ryzen 7 7800X3D is the most powerful gaming CPU right now.
