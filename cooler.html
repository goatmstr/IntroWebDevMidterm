Cooler

When electronic components such as the CPU and GPU take in lots of power and work heavy workloads, they get very hot. A cooling system is needed to ensure the part doesn't get too hot and end up causing permanent damage to hardware. The GPU comes with its own built in heatsink and fan but the CPU doesn't. That is why it is crucial to install a cooling system for the CPU when building a PC. Nowadays, this usually comes in the form of either an air cooling system or a liquid cooling system. Liquid cooling costs more but is usually better at keeping a lower temperature whereas air cooling is cheaper and easier to maintain but performs worse. For high end CPUs and heavy multicore workloads, liquid cooling is recommended. Liquid cooling has been greatly simplified with modern day all in one (AIO) coolers that can be purchased and installed with ease.
